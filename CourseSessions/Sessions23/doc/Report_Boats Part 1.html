What is this for?

This summarizes our findings for the Boats (A) Part 1. For this exercise, we began by determining the number of factors to consider. Based on trial and error, we arrived at a conclusion that 7 is the best number of factors. We reached this conclusion based on the eigenvalues of the factors (1-5 were above 1; the location of the elbow; and the cumulative percentage of explained variance. 7 factors explains 58.65% of the variance, as show in the tables below. In additon, we believe that 7 factors best fit the story told by our factors. The names of these factors and the data we used to arrive at these factors are described below.

Names of factors

  1. Hobby and adventure

  2. Passion and committment

  3. Status seekers

  4. Brand and quality

  5. Frugality and functionality

  6. Do-it-yourself (tinkerers)

  7. Customers who shop around
